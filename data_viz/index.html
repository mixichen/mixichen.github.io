  • A vaping outbreak caused 42 deaths

    Since March 31, 2019, there has been 2,172 cases of vaping-related lung diseases outbreak. As of November 13, 2019, 42 deaths has been confirmed. Over 16% of the patients are under 18.

  • The cause of disease has yet to be identified

    While Vitamin E acetate and THC have been identified by the CDC as the substances of concern in this outbreak, the definitive cause of the disease is still under investigation.




  • Anyone who is using e-cigarrete could be subject to the illness. The 3.05 million high school students and 570,000 middle school students currently using e-cigarettes are especially at risk.

  • In fact, vaping addiction has been on the rise.

    Each year more teenagers are adopting e-smoking and it's rising at an alarming rate. Since 2018, there has been an avergae rate of 18% increase in vaping among high-school students. By this rate, in 3 years more than half of high-school students will be vaping e-cigarette in some form or another.



  • But why is vaping so popular?

    According to CDC, the number one reason for vaping is "influence from family and friends." However, "preference and affinity for e-cigarette flavors" has also caught up in the last year to become a top reason for vaping among high-schoolers.

  • It's important to take actions, now

    Now that the reasons for vaping is clear, the CDC advises that parents "set a good example by being tobacco-free and ensure that your kid is not exposed to the secondhand emissions from any tobacco products, including e-cigarettes."

    Additionally, We believe that there should be an increased awareness created around this subject in the following ways:

        1. Young people should be educated about the outbreak of deadly diseases possible through use of e-cigarettes.

        2. Parents should conduct a serious discussion around the vaping behaviour and educate their kids about why e-cigarettes are harmful to them.

        3. Make youth health a compulsory and participatory subject in high-school curriculum where a significant focus in also given to vaping behavior.
